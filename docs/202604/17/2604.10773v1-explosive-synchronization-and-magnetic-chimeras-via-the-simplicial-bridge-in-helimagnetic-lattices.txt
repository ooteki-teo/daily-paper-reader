## Explosive Synchronization and Magnetic Chimeras via the Simplicial Bridge in Helimagnetic Lattices 

Alok Yadav 1

> 1

Department of Physics, Anugrah Memorial College, Magadh University, Gaya, Bihar 823001, India 

(Dated: April 14, 2026) The macroscopic dynamics of topological defects in magnetic materials are traditionally mod-eled using pairwise interactions, universally yielding continuous thermodynamic phase transitions. In this work, we extend the helimagnetic continuum model to include multi-spin biquadratic ex-change. Using the Karpman-Solov’ev adiabatic perturbation theory, we rigorously map the non-linear Landau-Lifshitz partial differential equations onto a generalized Kuramoto network operating on a simplicial complex. This “Simplicial Bridge” reveals that higher-order, triadic phase couplings induce explosive, first-order synchronization transitions characterized by a massive bistable hystere-sis loop. Furthermore, spectral analysis of these networks on bipartite honeycomb lattices demon-strates the evasion of geometric frustration and the emergence of macroscopic magnetic chimera states—spontaneous spatial domains of coexisting frozen and fluctuating spin textures. We discuss the physical manifestation of these states in strongly correlated van der Waals heterostructures and their potential application in reconfigurable magnonic reservoir computing. 

I. INTRODUCTION 

The macroscopic magnetic properties of condensed matter systems are fundamentally governed by the in-terplay of competing quantum mechanical exchange in-teractions. In helimagnets, the absence of structural in-version symmetry or the presence of magnetic frustration induces a spontaneous breaking of continuous rotational symmetry, leading to the formation of incommensurate, long-range spiral or chiral spin textures [1, 2]. The tem-poral evolution of the magnetization is strictly governed by the nonlinear Landau-Lifshitz (LL) equation [3]. Due to the inherent nonlinearity and dispersion embedded in the LL equation, the helimagnetic continuum supports a rich spectrum of localized, non-perturbative excitations known as magnetic solitons, including bright-dark kinks and skyrmions [4, 5]. Historically, the theoretical framework surrounding multi-soliton interactions has been confined to the di-lute limit, where spatial overlap is approximated strictly as a dyadic (two-body) phenomenon [13]. However, modern experimental and technological paradigms—such as dense chiral lattices and strongly correlated twisted Moir´ e superlattices—operate in regimes where four-spin scalar interactions become prominent [6]. In recent years, the discovery of intrinsic two-dimensional van der Waals magnets has further highlighted the critical role of higher-order interactions, such as biquadratic exchange, in stabilizing exotic topological states at finite tempera-tures [7]. This remains a highly active experimental fron-tier, with recent studies demonstrating how biquadratic exchange and selective ion intercalation actively tune macroscopic phase transitions in 2D antiferromagnets like MPS 3 and CrPS 4 [8, 9]. Concurrently, the application of simplicial complexes to nonlinear dynamics has surged. Recent theoret-ical breakthroughs have established that higher-order network structures strictly break interaction symmetry, driving robust explosive synchronization in coupled phase oscillators and biological networks [10, 11]. Yet, despite these advances in complex systems theory, a unified an-alytical approach capable of capturing higher-order con-tinuum interactions within the magnetic domain is miss-ing from the literature. In this paper, we bridge this gap by constructing an ex-act analytical mapping—the Simplicial Bridge—that re-duces the high-dimensional partial differential equations of multi-soliton collisions into coupled oscillator networks on hypergraphs. We demonstrate that these higher-order topological interactions are the fundamental prerequisite for explosive phase synchronization and the stabilization of macroscopic magnetic chimera states [12] in struc-turally homogeneous materials.

III. NETWORK TOPOLOGY OF MAGNETIC LATTICES 

To analyze the collective dynamics, we consider heli-magnetic solitons pinned to a bipartite honeycomb struc-ture, ubiquitous in transition metal dichalcogenides. 

A. Chiral Symmetry and Dirac Points 

The adjacency matrix A of the bipartite lattice as-sumes a block-off-diagonal form. The spectrum of A

possesses perfect chiral symmetry: if μ is an eigenvalue, then −μ is also an eigenvalue. Consequently, the den-sity of states exhibits Dirac cones in momentum space, 

ρ(λ) ∝ | λ − 3|. This structural depletion of the phase-space volume near the center of the spectrum fundamen-tally alters the specific heat capacity and shifts the crit-ical coupling threshold Kc required for macroscopic syn-chronization compared to standard non-bipartite lattices. 3

B. Frustration-Free Antiferromagnetic Locking 

For defects with opposite chiral charges ( σiσj = −1), the effective bilinear phase coupling is antiferromagnetic (K < 0). On a bipartite lattice, a local gauge transforma-tion ( θj = ϕj −π for the B sublattice) maps the antiferro-magnetic Kuramoto model exactly to the ferromagnetic case. This proves that, unlike the geometric frustration seen in triangular lattices, the helimagnet will uncondi-tionally phase-lock into a stable, macroscopic staggered 

π-phase state.

The most profound physical consequence of this bista-bility is the spontaneous breaking of dynamical symme-try in a structurally homogeneous material. The mean-field theory predicts a global thermodynamic bistability (the system resolves entirely to either R ≈ 1 or R ≈ 0). However, the introduction of spatial non-locality in the 4physical 2D lattice arrests this global phase separation. Pinned by the finite interaction radius, the helimagnet partitions into spatially distinct domains: a “frozen” core of perfectly phase-locked solitons (a magnonic crystal) stably coexisting with a “fluctuating” sea of incoherent defects (a spin liquid). This represents a macroscopic magnetic chimera state, visualized via numerical integra-tion in Fig. 3. While intrinsic biquadratic exchange is typically weaker than bilinear exchange, specific experi-mental conditions—such as extreme substrate strain or the magic-angle tuning of Moir´ e superlattices—can ar-tificially amplify the effective K2/K 1 ratio, pushing the material into the explosive regime necessary to stabilize these domains. Detecting these dynamical domains requires experi-mental techniques sensitive to temporal phase coherence. In real-space Lorentz Transmission Electron Microscopy (LTEM), the synchronized domains will appear as rigid, high-contrast lattices, while the fluctuating domains will blur into a time-averaged diffuse background. In mo-mentum space, Ferromagnetic Resonance (FMR) spec-troscopy will reveal a sharp Kittel mode absorption peak (the synchronized breather) superimposed atop a broad, heavily damped continuum background. Frustrated 2D van der Waals magnets (e.g., Fe 3GeTe 2) and twisted Moir´ e superlattices are prime material candidates for hosting these states due to their strong, tunable effec-tive multi-spin interactions.  

> VI. CONCLUSION

By bridging the nonlinear continuous dynamics of the Landau-Lifshitz equation with higher-order network topology, we have uncovered a novel mechanism for ex-plosive synchronization in quantum materials. The Sim-plicial Bridge mathematically proves that multi-spin in-teractions generate triadic network couplings, which are the fundamental prerequisite for stabilizing magnetic chimera states. The dynamic boundary of these chimera states acts as a tunable, nonreciprocal interface for spin waves. By controlling the local parameters to shift the system across the explosive synchronization boundary, these magnetic topologies offer a native, hardware-level substrate for reconfigurable magnonic reservoir comput-ing.
