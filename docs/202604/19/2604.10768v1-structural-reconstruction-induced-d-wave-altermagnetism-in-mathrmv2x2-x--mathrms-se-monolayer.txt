# Structural Reconstruction Induced d-wave Altermagnetism in V2X2

# (X = S , Se ) monolayer 

Geethanjali S and Sasmita Mohakud *

Department of Physics, School of Advanced Sciences, Vellore Institute of Technology, Vellore – 632014, Tamil Nadu, India 

## April 14, 2026 

Altermagnetism, featuring momentum-dependent spin splitting without relativistic effects, holds promise for next gen-eration spintronic applications. In this study, we investigate the momentum-dependent spin splitting in the electronic band structure of a reconstructed V2X2 (X = S , Se ) lattice, achieved by introducing chalcogen cluster vacancies in trigonal V X 2 (X = S , Se ) monolayer. The reconstructed structure forms an inverse Lieb lattice of vanadium atoms, comprising two magnetic sublattices related by C4 lattice rotational symmetry and C2 magnetic symmetry, resulting in zero net magnetization despite the breaking of time-reversal ( T ) and combined inversion time-reversal ( PT ) sym-metries. The electronic structure exhibits strongly anisotropic spin splitting on the Fermi surface, pronounced along 

Γ−X and Γ−Y and vanishing near M point, revealing symmetry-enforced nodal features. The spin splitting follows a fourfold angular modulation consistent with dx2−y2 -type altermagnetism, while the real-space spin density exhibits a corresponding d-wave pattern localized on vanadium sites. Our findings demonstrate that vacancy-driven reconstruc-tion provides an effective route realizing two-dimensional d-wave altermagnets, opening a new avenue for advanced spintronic technologies. Altermagnetism constitutes a recently identified mag-netic ordering characterized by momentum-dependent spin splitting in electronic bands, driven by crystal symmetry-enforced spin–momentum locking (CSML). 1–4 

In conventional antiferromagnets, spin-opposite sublat-tices are related by lattice translations ( τa) or spatial inversion ( P). However, in altermagnets these sublat-tices are connected via proper or improper rotational symmetries that may be symmorphic or nonsymmor-phic. 5 Consequently, time-reversal symmetry T breaks down, and combined antiunitary symmetries like T τa

or T P also fail to restore it. This fundamental sym-metry breaking produces the defining CSML and char-acteristic momentum-dependent spin splitting of alter-magnetic order. These spin-split bands enable uncon-ventional transport and optical responses, including the anomalous Hall effect (AHE), 6, 7 magneto-optical Kerr ef-fect (MOKE), 8, 9 efficient spin-transfer torque, and giant magnetoresistance. 10, 11 Experimental signatures, notably pronounced anisotropic spin splitting have been observed in M nT e through first-principles calculations and angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy (ARPES). 12–14

In this work, we employ chalcogen-cluster vacancy-induced lattice reconstruction as a powerful strategy to engineer an altermagnetic phase in two-dimensional ma-terials. Our approach starts with a pristine trigonal V X 2

(X = S , Se ) monolayer, depicted in Fig. 1. We introduce a targeted chain of chalcogen atoms removal, forming a distinctive eight-membered atomic ring vacancy con-figuration. Upon full structural optimization the system relaxes into a stable monoclinic structure, accompanied by a fundamental crystal-symmetry reduction stabilizing the altermagnetic phase. The first-principles calculations confirm the stability of the materials and yield detailed electronic structure predictions, providing a clear theoret-ical roadmap for experimental viability and verification of these 2D altermagnet. 

> Results and discussion
> Structural properties and stability analysis

We consider the two-dimensional trigonal crystal struc-ture of vanadium dichalcogenides, V X 2 (X = S , Se ), 29, 30 

shown in Fig. 1(a, b). The pristine monolayer features a characteristic X-V-X sandwich configuration, where each vanadium atom sits at the center of an octahedron formed by six chalcogen atoms. This octahedral coordination not only stabilizes the overall trigonal lattice symmetry but also governs the electronic structure, where the va-lence states are predominantly derived from vanadium d-orbitals, giving rise to metallic behavior and intrinsic fer-romagnetic ordering. To engineer the electronic and mag-netic properties, we introduce targeted chalcogen-cluster vacancies into the V X 2 monolayer. > Electronic properties

To elucidate the electronic and magnetic properties, we compute spin-polarized band structure and density of states (DOS) for the reconstructed V2X2 (X = S , Se ), and present in Fig. 4. Band crossing as well as finite density of states at Fermi level ( EF) confirms the metal-lic character of both the systems. In addition, a defining signature emerges along high-symmetric points X and Y (see Fig. 4(a), (c)). We observe a pronounced momentum-dependent spin splitting that alternates sign along differ-ent crystal momentum directions kx and ky . This be-havior originates from the reconstructed monoclinic point group C2h = {E, C 2, P, σ h}. In the magnetic phase, the parent crystallographic symmetries, namely the C2 rota-tion about the unique x-axis, spatial inversion P through the lattice center, and their combined operation σh re-main preserved and leave the collinear magnetic configu-ration invariant. Time-reversal symmetry T breaks spon-taneously due to the finite magnetic moment on vana-dium atoms. However, the combined antiunitary symme-try PT , whose presence guarantees Kramers degeneracy through (PT )2 = −1, is explicitly absent in this mag-netic space group. This fundamental symmetry breaking permits non-relativistic spin splitting E↑(k)̸ = E↓(k) at general k-points except high-symmetry nodes, where the spin states are degenerate. As discussed earlier, the vana-dium atoms in the reconstructed lattice form an inverse Lieb network with square unitcell. The vanadium atoms occupying edge positions in this lattice network experi-ence different local environment along X and Y directions and related by C4 lattice rotational symmetry rather than translation. This results in the localization of opposite magnetic moments on these magnetic sites making them inequivalent. The similar spin splitting behavior has also been observed in 2D altermagnet RuF 4.17

> Summary

We systematically investigate the vacancy-driven lattice reconstruction in trigonal V X 2 (X = S , Se ) monolay-ers, and find that it leads to the formation of stable mon-oclinic V2X2 structures. The stability of the system is supported by negative formation energies, the absence of imaginary phonon modes throughout the Brillouin zone, and stable ab initio molecular dynamics at 300 K, in-dicating experimental viability. Our first principle re-sults reveal that these reconstructed structures exhibit al-termagnetic behavior characterized by momentum depen-dent spin splitting in the absence of spin-orbit coupling with a clear sign reversal between symmetry related k-points. The momentum resolved spin splitting and its real space manifestaion have been investigated through the analysis of Fermi surface and spin density calcula-tions, respectively. The Fermi surface displays a char-acteristic dx2−y2 -like anisotropy which indicates that the reconstructed structures host a stable d-wave altermagnet. In addition, the real space spin density is localized on two inequivalent sublattices with opposite polarization, result-ing in complete spin compensation and zero net magne-tization. Our study suggests that these chalcogen cluster vacancies could be an effective way to engineer the sym-metry to realize 2D altermagnetism. Beyond structural and electronic characterization, further studies on spin-selective transport including anomalous Hall effect, spin Hall conductivity, and THz spin dynamics would be help-ful to establish V2X2 as a viable candidate for dissipation-less spintronic applications. 

> Methods

To investigate the structural, electronic, and magnetic properties of the system, we perform first-principles cal-culations within the framework of density functional the-ory (DFT) as implemented in Quantum ESPRESSO pack-age. 35 We consider the generalized gradient approxi-mation (GGA) with the Perdew-Burke-Ernzerhof (PBE) functional 36 to treat the exchange-correlation effects in our calculations. A plane-wave basis set is used for our calculation with a kinetic energy cutoff of 70 Ry. We con-sider an energy threshold of 10 −8 Ry as the convergence criteria for the self-consistent field (SCF) calculations en-suring high numerical accuracy. Brillouin zone sampling uses a Γ-centered 12 × 12 × 1 k-point mesh, paired with Methfessel-Paxton smearing of 0.07 Ry to optimize con-vergence. The structural relaxations are performed until the residual forces on each atom fall below 0.02 Ry/Bohr. To eliminate interactions between periodic replicas, we introduce a vacuum layer of 20 Å along the out-of-plane 

z-direction. To study the dynamical stability, we com-pute phonon dispersion using the Vienna ab initio Sim-ulation Package (VASP) 37, 38 through density-functional perturbation theory (DFPT). 39 The thermal stability of the reconstructed structures are further assessed via ab initio 

molecular dynamics (AIMD) simulations implemented in VASP. The simulation is performed using a Nosé-Hoover thermostat which controls the temperature at 300 K with 2 fs timesteps over 5000 steps (10 ps total duration), pro-viding robust finite-temperature validation.
