(Dated: April 1, 2026) We study localization in a quasiperiodic spinful antiferromagnetic Hubbard ring within aself-consistent Hartree–Fock framework, emphasizing the interplay of quasiperiodicity, staggered Zeeman-field-induced antiferromagnetic order, and electron correlations. Localization properties are characterized through inverse participation ratios, normalized participation ratios, and multifractal-ity, and are consistently supported by a broad class of real-space mean-field observables, including double occupancy, density fluctuations, local entropy, spin-density-wave (SDW) order, and other re-lated correlation measures. We uncover a pronounced nonmonotonic evolution of localization with interaction strength, featuring an intermediate regime marked by enhanced localization, strong spa-tial inhomogeneity, and magnetic ordering, followed by a re-entrant tendency toward delocalization at stronger interaction regime. Phase diagrams constructed from complementary localization and mean-field indicators reveal extended, localized, and critical regimes governed by the interplay of quasiperiodicity and interactions. Furthermore, real-time wave-packet dynamics of eigenstates pro-vide direct evidence of ballistic spreading, confinement, and re-entrant transport, in agreement with the underlying spectral characteristics. These results establish a unified framework where diverse mean-field observables and dynamical probes consistently capture correlation-driven localization phenomena in quasiperiodic systems. 

I. INTRODUCTION 

Understanding the fate of quantum states in the pres-ence of disorder and interactions remains a central prob-lem in condensed-matter physics. In noninteracting sys-tems, disorder can suppress transport through Anderson localization[ 1], while quasiperiodic potentials provide a deterministic route to localization without randomness, exhibiting sharp transitions between extended and local-ized states [ 2, 3]. Quasiperiodic lattices further host criti-cal phases characterized by multifractal eigenstates, lying intermediate between fully extended and fully localized regimes. These features make quasiperiodic systems [ 4–

29 ] an ideal platform for exploring localization phenom-ena beyond conventional disordered models [ 30 –33 ]. The role of interactions in such systems, however, remains far less understood. Interactions [ 34 –39 ] can either enhance localization by amplifying spatial inho-mogeneities or promote delocalization through screening and spectral restructuring. In quasiperiodic settings, this competition is particularly subtle, as interactions act on a background that is already spatially modulated in a deterministic manner. As a result, interaction-driven effects can be highly nonmonotonic, giving rise to re-entrant behavior, coexistence of localized and extended states, and extended critical regimes. Identifying the

An additional layer of complexity arises when spin de-grees of freedom are included. Spin-dependent potentials, such as Zeeman fields or staggered magnetic textures, ex-plicitly break spin symmetry and enable the possibility of spin-selective localization and transport. When com-bined with interactions, even small spin-density imbal-ances can be amplified through self-consistent mean-field potentials, leading to subtle yet physically meaningful spin asymmetries in the spatial structure of eigenstates. Such effects are of direct relevance to spin-dependent transport, spin caloritronics, and engineered quantum systems where spin-resolved control is possible. Motivated by these considerations, we study a spin-ful Hubbard ring with quasiperiodically modulated hop-ping subjected to a staggered spin-dependent Zeeman field. The system is treated within a self-consistent Hartree–Fock framework, which captures interaction-induced renormalization of the effective single-particle spectrum while remaining computationally tractable for large system sizes. Although mean-field in nature, this approach is well suited for uncovering interaction-driven spectral reconstruction, localization trends, and spin-dependent effects, and provides a controlled starting point for exploring dynamical behavior. The central questions we address are as follows: How do interactions reshape the localization properties of a quasiperiodic spinful system in the presence of a stag-gered Zeeman field? Under what conditions do interac-tions induce localization, restore delocalization, or stabi-lize critical multifractal regimes? By systematically exploring these ques-tions across a wide parameter space, we provide a unified picture of interaction-driven localization, spin asymme-try, and dynamical transport in quasiperiodic Hubbard systems. The key findings of this work are as follows: (a) the emergence of an intermediate localized regime with increasing Hubbard interaction strength U , (b) the restoration of an extended phase at higher values of U ,(c) a contrasting localization behavior between edge and bulk states, where for lower U the edge states remain delocalized while the bulk states are relatively localized, whereas for larger U the edge states become compara-tively localized and the bulk states tend toward delocal-ization, (d) the existence of the intermediate localized phase and the reentrant delocalized phase is further sub-stantiated through the analysis of fractal dimensions and relevant mean-field order parameters, and (e) these char-acteristic features are consistently preserved in the dy-namical behavior of the system. In Fig. 6, we analyze the interaction dependence of the spin-resolved differences ∆AIPR and ∆ANPR. At weak interaction strengths, both quantities remain nearly zero, reflecting the fact that spin-up and spin-down electrons experience almost identical effective mean-field potentials and thus exhibit similar spatial distributions. In this regime, the eigenstates are predominantly extended, and the influence of the interaction-induced Hartree term on spin differentiation is minimal. As U is increased into the intermediate interaction regime, both ∆AIPR and ∆ANPR develop pronounced maxima. This enhance-ment coincides with the onset of interaction-driven local-ization observed in Fig. 5, indicating that localized eigen-states are significantly more sensitive to spin-dependent fluctuations of the mean-field potential. In this regime, small differences in the local spin densities are ampli-fied by the Hubbard interaction, leading to an imbal-ance in the spatial confinement of spin-up and spin-down states. Consequently, localization acts as a magnifying mechanism for spin asymmetry, even though the overall magnitude of the difference remains small. Upon further increasing U , both ∆AIPR and ∆ANPR gradually de-crease and approach zero again. This behavior reflects the restoration of a more homogeneous mean-field en-vironment at strong coupling, where interaction effects dominate over quasiperiodic modulation and suppress spin-dependent spatial differentiation. The nonmono-tonic dependence of ∆AIPR and ∆ANPR on U there-fore establishes a direct connection between correlation-induced localization and spin-resolved spatial asymmetry in the interacting quasiperiodic system.  

> D. Variation of IPR (NPR) with eigenstates

In Fig. 7, we present the eigenstate-resolved inverse participation ratio (IPR) and normalized participation ratio (NPR) for λ = 0.8 and hz = 0.8 at four rep-resentative interaction strengths. For weak interaction (U = 1), the spectrum is predominantly extended, as reflected by low IPR and high NPR values, except for states near the center of the spectrum that exhibit en-hanced localization. Upon increasing the interaction to 

U = 5, interaction-induced correlations substantially re-shape the spatial character of the eigenstates: the sys-tem displays a mixed regime in which localized states emerge in the central part of the spectrum, while neigh-boring states show partial delocalization. This nonuni-form localization pattern persists and becomes more pro-nounced at U = 7, where a larger fraction of eigen-states exhibits stronger spatial confinement. At strong coupling ( U = 9), the localization profile undergoes a qualitative inversion: the edge states become strongly localized, whereas a broad portion of the spectrum recov-ers extended character. The spectrum retains a mirror-symmetric structure about its center for all interaction strengths, reflecting the underlying lattice and spin sym-metries. Overall, these results demonstrate a nonmono-tonic and spectrally selective evolution of localization driven by the interplay between quasiperiodicity, Zeeman splitting, and interaction effects. Figure 8 presents the same set of four subplots for 

λ = 0 .5 and hz = 0 .4. In this case, a pronounced mirror-symmetric structure of the eigenstate profiles is observed across all interaction strengths. Unlike the higher-λ case discussed earlier, the spectral inversion of localization characteristics, previously seen at large U is absent here, indicating a weaker interaction-driven redistribution of localized and extended states in this parameter regime. We further observe that for most eigenstates the IPR (NPR) of the spin-up and spin-down sectors overlap almost exactly, indicating nearly identical localization properties for the two spin channels. However, for a sub-set of eigenstates this overlap breaks down, manifested as distinct deviations that appear, for instance, as pro-nounced spikes (red color) in the IPR spectrum. These features signal eigenstates for which the spatial local-ization characteristics of the two spin components differ quantitatively, reflecting a state-dependent spin asym-metry induced by interactions. Such deviations, signal-ing state-dependent spin asymmetry in localization, are more prominent in Fig. 8 than in Fig. 7.

> F. Different mean field parameters with U

To further quantify the interaction-driven restructur-ing of the electronic states, we examine the variance of the local site occupations, defined as Var sites = 〈n↑〉 (1 − 〈 n↑〉) + 〈n↓〉 (1 − 〈 n↓〉) , (15) where 〈nσ〉 denotes the spin-resolved mean density ob-tained from the self-consistent Hartree solution. In Fig. 12 (a), we present the interaction dependence of the density fluctuation Var( n), obtained by averag-ing Var sites over all lattice sites. In the weakly interact-10 

ing regime, Var( n) assumes relatively large values, re-flecting nearly homogeneous charge distributions associ-ated with extended eigenstates; correspondingly, the av-erage inverse participation ratio (AIPR) remains low. As the Hubbard interaction U is increased, Var( n) initially decreases, following a trend opposite to that of AIPR. However, upon entering an intermediate interaction win-dow, a subset of eigenstates undergoes localization driven by the combined effects of quasiperiodicity and the self-consistent Hartree potential. This interaction-induced lo-calization gives rise to pronounced spatial density mod-ulations, enhancing site-to-site charge fluctuations and leading to a noticeable increase in Var( n). The simulta-neous enhancement of Var( n) and AIPR in this regime establishes a direct correspondence between density inho-mogeneity and eigenstate localization. At larger values of U , the effective mean-field potential becomes smoother again, and the system returns to a weakly inhomoge-neous or extended regime, where both Var( n) and AIPR are suppressed. The resulting nonmonotonic evolution of Var( n) with U therefore closely tracks the behavior of AIPR, demonstrating that density fluctuations provide an independent and complementary probe of interaction-driven localization in the quasiperiodic Hubbard model. We further investigate the interaction-driven evolution of the local electronic structure through the site-averaged local entropy, defined as 

Sloc = − [n↑ ln( n↑) + n↓ ln( n↓)] , (19) with n↑ and n↓ obtained from the self-consistent Hartree solution. Figure 12 (f) displays the spatially averaged quantity 〈Sloc 〉, denoted by S, as a function of the interac-tion strength U . At weak coupling, S assumes relatively large values, reflecting nearly homogeneous spin densi-ties and a high degree of local configurational uncertainty characteristic of extended eigenstates. As the interaction strength U is increased into an intermediate regime, the system undergoes a qualitative reorganization driven by the interplay of quasiperiodicity and interaction-induced Hartree fields. In this regime, strong site-dependent den-sity modulations develop, leading to pronounced spatial variations of both n↑ and n↓. These inhomogeneities en-hance local charge and spin fluctuations rather than sup-pressing them, resulting in a marked increase of the site-averaged local entropy S. The entropy peak coincides with the maxima observed in Var( n) and D, as well as with enhanced AIPR and the emergence of a finite single-particle gap, establishing the intermediate-U window as the regime of strongest correlation effects. Upon further increasing U , the effective mean-field potential becomes smoother and the system crosses over into a weakly inho-mogeneous or re-extended regime, where density fluctua-tions are reduced. Consequently, S decreases again, mir-roring the behavior of Var( n) and D. The resulting non-monotonic dependence of the local entropy on U there-fore provides an entropic fingerprint of interaction-driven localization and spectral reconstruction in the quasiperi-odic Hubbard model.

> IV. CONCLUSION

In conclusion, we have systematically investigated interaction-driven localization, spin asymmetry, multi-fractality, and nonequilibrium dynamics in a quasiperi-odic spinful Hubbard ring subjected to a staggered spin-dependent Zeeman field within a self-consistent mean-field framework. By combining IPR-resolved spectral analysis, spin-dependent localization diagnostics, aver-aged participation measures, fractal-dimension charac-terization, real-space mean-field observables, and real-time wave-packet dynamics, we have demonstrated that the interplay of quasiperiodicity, Zeeman splitting, and Hubbard interaction produces a highly nonmonotonic and spectrally selective evolution of electronic states. Starting from a predominantly extended regime at weak coupling, the system undergoes a pronounced interaction-induced restructuring in an intermediate-U

window, marked by enhanced localization, spin-resolved asymmetry, spectral gap opening, strong density inhomo-geneity, and elevated local entropy, before exhibiting a re-entrant tendency toward delocalization at strong cou-pling driven by Hartree renormalization. The phase di-agrams constructed from the combined localization pa-rameter η, the extrema of the inverse participation ra-tio, and the average fractal dimension D2 reveal the coexistence of extended, localized, and critical multi-fractal regimes, with their stability strongly controlled by the quasiperiodic strength and Zeeman field. While the majority of eigenstates retain nearly identical local-ization properties for the two spin sectors, a subset of states in the intermediate regime displays enhanced spin-dependent localization, reflecting the amplification of small spin-density imbalances by interactions in spatially confined states. These static signatures are fully cor-roborated by real-time dynamical probes, where ballistic spreading, strong confinement, and re-emergent trans-port are observed in direct correspondence with the un-derlying eigenstate properties. Taken together, our re-sults establish a unified physical picture in which inter-actions act as a dual agent, capable of both inducing and suppressing localization in quasiperiodic systems, while enabling controlled spin-selective effects through stag-14 

gered Zeeman coupling. Our findings are directly rele-vant to engineered quantum materials, cold-atom optical lattices, and synthetic lattice platforms, and they pro-vide a robust foundation for future studies incorporating beyond-mean-field correlations, dissipation, or higher-dimensional geometries.
